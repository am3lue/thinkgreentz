Makutupora Microforest

Using the Miyawaki method and community watering rosters, we transformed a dusty verge into a thriving pocket forest. 2,400 native saplings, 32 species, and a living outdoor classroom for nearby schools.

  • Outcome: 92% survival after first dry season
  • Co‚Äëbenefits: Cooler walkways, pollinator habitat, and compost hub for leaf litter
  • Partners: Ward office, three schools, and youth volunteers

With farmer field schools, we introduced nitrogen‚Äëfixing trees and contour hedgerows. Soil moisture increased, wind damage decreased, and yields stabilized through erratic rains.
