About Us – THINK GREEN

A youth-led movement for a cleaner, greener Tanzania

Born in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, THINK GREEN transforms the belief that “our environment is our home” into action. What started as a small student club tackling local litter has grown into a vibrant, community-driven initiative empowering youth, children, women, and entire communities to create a sustainable future.

Youth Environmental Leadership

Through events like ECOLEADERS RISE 2025, we inspire young changemakers with workshops, creative forums, and innovation sessions, turning their ideas into impactful climate action.

School Outreach & Education

Our school-based environmental clubs bring tree planting, climate education, and eco-literacy to students, fostering green spaces that enhance learning, food security, and community well-being.

Community-Led Conservation

We design community-based projects—from tree-planting campaigns to cleanups—that provide shade, nourishment, and dignity while uniting people to protect their environment.

Events, Advocacy & Recognition

From environmental festivals to earning nominations like Environmental Champion at the TEYA Awards, we amplify youth voices on national stages, proving young people are powerful agents of change.

Partnerships for Impact

We collaborate with partners like @globalplatform_tanzania, @climate.fresk.tanzania, and @africaforsdgs to scale our efforts and create transformative opportunities.

Our Vision

A sustainable Tanzania powered by bold young leaders and empowered communities—a world where environmental responsibility is a way of life. At THINK GREEN TZ, every small act, from planting a tree to sparking a conversation, fuels global change.

🌍 Community Engagement

We believe change starts in our own neighborhoods. Through hands-on activities, THINK GREEN brings people together to create cleaner, healthier, and more sustainable communities. Our community engagement initiatives foster collaboration, encouraging youth, women, and local leaders to participate in tree-planting drives, clean-up campaigns, and waste management education to build a sustainable future.

  • Outcome: 92% survival after first dry season
  • Co‑benefits: Cooler walkways, pollinator habitat, and compost hub for leaf litter
  • Partners: Ward office, three schools, and youth volunteers

🌳 Tree Planting

We organize tree-planting drives in schools, villages, and urban areas to restore ecosystems, combat climate change, and provide cleaner air. Each tree planted is a symbol of hope and a legacy for future generations, contributing to our broader community engagement and grassroots conservation efforts.

  • Outcome: Thousands of trees planted across multiple regions
  • Co‑benefits: Enhanced air quality, increased shade, and restored ecosystems
  • Partners: Local schools, community groups, and environmental NGOs

🧹 Community Clean-Ups

By mobilizing youth, women, and local leaders, we hold regular clean-up campaigns to tackle waste in public spaces. These activities, part of our community engagement efforts, not only beautify our surroundings but also raise awareness on proper waste management and responsibility, fostering sustainable communities.

  • Outcome: Cleaner public spaces and reduced litter
  • Co‑benefits: Increased community pride and environmental awareness
  • Partners: Local governments, youth groups, and volunteers

♻️ Waste Management Education

Alongside clean-ups, we provide practical training on waste sorting, recycling, and reducing plastic use. This empowers communities to turn environmental challenges into opportunities for sustainable living, aligning with our mission to promote community engagement and responsible waste management practices.

  • Outcome: Increased recycling rates and reduced plastic waste
  • Co‑benefits: Empowered communities and reduced environmental pollution
  • Approach: Hands-on workshops and community-led initiatives

🌱 Grassroots Conservation

We work hand-in-hand with local communities to protect wetlands, forests, and other vital ecosystems. These efforts, combined with tree-planting and clean-up campaigns, build a sense of ownership and highlight the importance of preserving biodiversity close to home, fostering sustainable communities through grassroots conservation.

  • Outcome: Protected ecosystems and increased biodiversity
  • Co‑benefits: Strengthened community ties and preserved natural habitats
  • Partners: Local conservation groups and community leaders

Questions we hear a lot

Are donations tax-deductible?
Donations directly support our non-profit projects, community programs, and reforestation efforts. Depending on your country’s tax regulations, contributions may qualify for tax deduction. Each receipt includes our registration details so you can check with your local authorities or accountant. Even if not deductible, every contribution fuels long-term impact on people and the planet.
How do you choose tree species?
We collaborate with local botanists, environmental experts, and community elders to select species best suited for each region. Our focus is on native and drought-tolerant trees that restore soil, conserve water, and provide food, shade, or income. Every planting plan balances biodiversity, climate resilience, and community benefit, avoiding invasive or non-sustainable species.
Can Thinny help my school or farm?
Yes! We partner with schools, farms, and community groups to design simple, affordable greening projects. First, we conduct a site walk and listen to your goals—whether shade for students, fruit for families, or soil recovery for fields. Then, we co-create a planting plan, provide seedlings, and guide your team through planting and care. We also offer training so your project thrives long after planting day.
Do you track impact over time?
Absolutely. After planting, we revisit sites at regular intervals to measure tree survival rates, soil health improvements, and community benefits. Our team also uses simple surveys and drone mapping where possible. The data feeds into open dashboards where anyone can track progress, suggest improvements, and even contribute their own local observations. Transparency is a key part of our work.
Who takes care of the trees after planting?
We don’t just plant and walk away. Every project is co-owned by the local community—whether schools, farmers, or families. We provide training on watering, mulching, and pest management, and set up caretaker groups. In some cases, we also provide water tanks, protective fencing, or organic compost to increase survival rates. Ownership ensures long-term responsibility and pride in the forest grown.
Can I volunteer with Thinny?
Yes! Volunteers are vital. Opportunities include tree planting, nursery care, community training, and digital support (like storytelling, photography, or data entry). Whether you join us in person at a planting site or contribute remotely, we’ll find a role that matches your skills and passion. Sign up on our website or email us directly to get started.
How do you make sure trees survive long-term?
Survival is more important than planting numbers. We use protective guards, water-saving techniques like drip irrigation and mulching, and train communities in ongoing care. We also plant during the rainy season whenever possible to give seedlings the best start. Our follow-up visits help identify and replant any losses, ensuring healthy forests rather than short-term numbers.
Where does Thinny work?
Our focus is in regions facing deforestation, soil erosion, and water scarcity, starting with communities in Tanzania and expanding gradually. Each site is selected with local leaders to ensure land rights are clear and long-term stewardship is possible. As we grow, we partner with other organizations across Africa and beyond to replicate the Thinny model.
How are donations spent?
We believe in transparency. About 80% of donations go directly to community planting programs, nursery development, training, and monitoring. The rest supports coordination, tools, and minimal administrative costs. We publish simple annual reports so supporters can see exactly where funds flow and what impact they create—both in numbers of trees and lives improved.
Can businesses or groups partner with Thinny?
Definitely. We design partnership programs for companies, NGOs, and community groups. Options include sponsoring planting days, co-branding campaigns, employee volunteer days, or offsetting carbon footprints. By working together, we multiply impact—restoring ecosystems while strengthening community livelihoods. Contact us for tailored partnership opportunities.
